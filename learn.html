In Spanish, you can tell who was doing what, and when, from the way the verb is conjugated. Click on one of the below to find out how to conjugate a verb in that tense.

Infinitive

The infinitive doesn't actually indicate who is doing what. The infinitive is the natural form of the verb - i.e. the verb in its original state.

In Spanish, verbs always have one of three endings: -ar, -er and -ir. The ending will help you to understand which way to conjugate the verb.

The part before the -ar, -er or -ir ending is the stem of the verb and is used for the basis of the conjugations. More often than not, the stem will remain the same - however, there are a significant number of stem-changing verbs and irregular verbs. We will cover those in each section.

Present Tense

The present tense tells you what is happening now (as opposed to the past or the future) - or things that happen regularly.
